Deep learning models

Three model families
behind smart screening

From semantic understanding, to contextual reasoning, to generation — the stages that took résumé screening beyond literal keywords.

// 01

Transformers & BERT

Semantic embeddings
Predictive AI

Represent a résumé and a job description as semantic vectors and measure similarity in meaning, not in words — the heart of moving past literal matching.

Applied: conSultantBERT (Lavi et al., 2021) trained on 270K résumé–job pairs, beating TF-IDF and matching across languages.
// 02

LLMs & RAG

Large language models
Generative AI

Reason about transferable skills, generate readable justifications for a ranking, and ground decisions in company data via retrieval-augmented generation.

Applied: "AI Hiring with LLMs" (arXiv 2025) — a multi-agent, explainable screening framework that separates extraction, matching and justification.
// 03

GAN & Diffusion

Generative data models
Generative AI

Generate synthetic résumés to fight data scarcity and demographic imbalance. A double-edged sword: biased inputs reproduce bias.

Applied: research combining GANs with LLMs to improve job recommendation (Du et al., 2024, AAAI).

The hard part

What efficiency
numbers hide

Screening people automatically is sensitive. High accuracy can conceal deep bias.

// 01

Learned bias

Amazon's tool favored men because it trained on historically male data, downgrading résumés mentioning "women's." A model doesn't invent bias — it reflects its data, more efficiently.

// 02

Explainability & gaming

Why was a candidate rejected? Explaining the decision is hard. Meanwhile applicants stuff keywords or inject hidden instructions (prompt injection) to fool the system.

// 03

Regulation & Arabic data

Rising laws (LL 144, EU AI Act) demand bias audits and transparency. Locally, scarce Arabic résumé data and NLP models are both a challenge and a research opportunity.

Next 5 years

Four shifts ahead

Most important locally: Saudi Arabia turning into a regional lab for skills-based, compliant intelligent hiring.

Agentic recruiters

Agents screen, interview and reach out semi-autonomously. The human shifts from "screener" to "supervisor who audits and overrides."

Skills-based hiring

Less reliance on formal credentials, more on proven skills — backed by taxonomies like ESCO and models like CareerBERT. This embodies the research question.

Explainable & compliant AI

As laws tighten, explainability and bias auditing become a competitive advantage, not a luxury.

Saudi Arabia as a regional lab

With Jadarat expanding, Arabic NLP maturing, and SDAIA & HUMAIN support, the Kingdom can lead locally compliant, skills-based hiring.
